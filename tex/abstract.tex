This paper presents a method that integrates tactile feedback from soft robotic fingers into imitation learning for manipulation. Simple ion-liquid sensors embedded in the fingers provide tactile signals directly to an Action Chunking with Transformers (ACT) model. We evaluate the method on grasping sheet-like flexible objects under visual occlusion and show robust performance even with simple sensors. These results indicate that combining soft robotic fingers with learning-based control can handle complex contact transitions and environmental variations.
